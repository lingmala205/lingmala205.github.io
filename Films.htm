Films

The Lord of the Rings (LOTR)

Peter Jackson's film trilogy based on Tolkien's books revitalized the high fantasy genre and revolutionized the motion picture industry. The films, The Fellowship of the Ring, The Two Towers, and The Return of the King, were filmed from 1999 to 2000 and released between 2001 and 2003, inclusive. With Howard Shore's unique and mesmerizing score, cutting-edge visual effects at the time, and spectacular cinematography, the LOTR film trilogy collectively won 17 Academy Awards out of 30 they were nominated for. The Return of the King won all 11 Academy Awards it was nominated for, a rare feat. Howard Shore won 'Best Original Score' twice for the first and third films in the trilogy, while also winning 'Best Original Song' for 'Into the West' with Annie Lennox and Fran Walsh. Jackson won 'Best Director' for The Return of the King, as well as 'Best Adapted Screenplay' with Fran Walsh and Philippa Boyens and 'Best Picture' with Barrie M. Osborne and Fran Walsh. The trilogy was also financially successful, with The Return of the King earning more than $1 billion at the box office. The films collectively made $2,917,506,956.

The Hobbit

Peter Jackson helmed the prequel trilogy of his critically and financially successful LOTR trilogy. Based on Tolkien's 1937 novel, Jackson reassembled most of his original crew and some of his original cast from the early 2000s. Howard Shore returned to compose the score. Ian McKellen returned as Gandalf, Hugo Weaving returned as Elrond, Sir Christopher Lee returned as Saruman, and Cate Blanchett returned as Lady Galadriel. An Unexpected Journey was released in 2012, The Desolation of Smaug was released in 2013, and The Battle of the Five Armies was released in 2014. While this trilogy is not as critically lauded as its predecessor trilogy, it nevertheless made $2.93 billion at the box office. An Unexpected Journey won a special Scientific and Engineering Academy Award in 2013.

The Chronicles Of Narnia

Andrew Adamson directed the 2005 adaptation of The Lion, the Witch, and The Wardrobe and the 2008 adaptation of Prince Caspian. Michael Apted directed the 2010 adaptation of The Voyage of the Dawn Treader. These three films have collectively made $1,580,364,900 at the box office. It is widely considered that the first film is the most well done of the three. In 2018, it was announced that Netflix would be making a TV adaptation of the original series by C.S. Lewis.
