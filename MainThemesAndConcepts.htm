Main Themes and Concepts

Magic

Magic is one of the most famous concepts used in high fantasy. Be they protagonists or antagonists, a plethora of characters wield supernatural powers and boast tremendous mystical strength. Magic is used prominently by wizards, mages, sorcerers, and even celestial beings. These unique powers may be used for attack, defense, healing and even learning. Physical places in high fantasy works may also possess magical power; ancient forests may have sentient trees, and majestic citadels could have spells cast over them for protection against external threats attempting to penetrate its ramparts, with the enchantments etched into their stone.

Wizards

Wizards, also called mages or sorcerers, are notable characters in high fantasy. They frequently use magic to solve problems and to aid in combat. Wizards are sometimes also able to traverse space and time and create entire dimensions outside of reality. Wizards have been portrayed as ancient, wise, scholarly and capable of controlling the weather and understanding animals to an extent. Saruman, an Istar (wizard) from The Hobbit and The Lord of the Rings books, is able to understand crows and makes lightning strike a snowy mountain in Peter Jackson's film adaptations.

Elves

Elves are inherently magical creatures, immortal and extremely powerful. They are usually portrayed as humanoids of divine origin who are in harmony with nature and having unmatched beauty. They have distinct, pointed ears which distinguish them from most other races in high fantasy. The elves created by J.R.R. Tolkien were the first children of Eru Ilúvatar, the Celestial being who created the world of Middle-earth. Tolkien's elves do not suffer from disease and are naturally immortal. Elves can also be portrayed as having their own languages, such as Sindarin and Quenya.
Dwarves
Dwarves are a race of short, humanoid creatures. They are often portrayed as dwelling deep in the mountains, working as expert blacksmiths, miners, and jewelers. However, they are highly capable of fierce combat, and are incredibly loyal to their allies. Dwarves in both The Chronicles of Narnia and The Hobbit and the Lord of the Rings served their leaders and allies faithfully, as seen with the dwarves that side with Aslan, and Gimli's strong friendship with Legolas, respectively.
Dragons
Dragons are famous creatures in high fantasy. Their abilities of breathing fire, flight, and sheer raw power have made them the one of the most recognizable mythical creatures of all time. Dragons are frequently being depicted as scaly, horned and flying lizard beings who wreak havoc on cities and empires, but can also be depicted as sentient beings who are compassionate towards other beings, such as dragons in many Asian mythologies.
Other Creatures
Ents, pictured here, are treelike sheperds of the forests in Middle-earth. Orcs are cruel, hideous, violent beings who appear in many works of high fantasy, doing their master's bidding. There are great eagles who save the protagonists from death, ghosts who haunt the mountains, and gods who rule over multiple universes and who even fight each other. The types of fantasy creatures that exist are only limited by one's imagination.
The Quest
In high fantasy, the 'good vs. evil' dichotomy is often employed to give the good protagonist a sense of purpose and defeat the sinister threats that lurk in the shadows. The protagonist's mission is usually a journey, or quest, which involves many members united by a common cause travelling far and wide to bring peace in their time.
World Building
For the vast majority of high fantasy works, the universe that inhabits the stories are as integral to them as the characters are. Magnificent, sprawling cities and legions of brave warriors defending them immediately conjures excitement and wonder simultaneously. The sleepy, charming villages of the Shire filled with sweet aromas and joyous laughter paints a delightful image in our minds. Reading is an adventure in itself, and when you read high fantasy, one believes that they dwell in the same world which they are reading about. Populating a story with a universe that gives eagerness, thrill, awe and many other emotions life defines the genius of many high fantasy writers.
